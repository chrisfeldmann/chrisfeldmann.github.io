Process

While every project is different, below is a brief outline of my ideal workflow process:

01. Collaborate

The first step is always collaboration between myself and the stakeholders. Here we try to articulate and define the brand and guiding principles of the product. Clearly and correctly identifying the problem or business challenge shapes the priorities within the product, how to measure success, and the overall goals that the stakeholders are trying to achieve with the product.

02. Discovery

This phase of the process is comprised of user and research activities. I conduct stakeholder interviews, analyse web and mobile usage data, and try to conduct surveys and contextual enquiry (interviewing potential users in the same location that they may use the product), and perform a competitive analysis.

03. Analysis

Here I strive to draw insights that I gained from the discovery phase. I use user personas and storyboards to further define who the user might be and what solutions they may want in a digital product. A simple exercise that I often use is "The user is [blank], who wants [blank]" - where I will insert realistic and specific users and wants.

04. Design

As an Interaction/User Experience Designer I am often responsible for shaping and communicating user experience strategies and design. I often serve as the team advisor responsible for defining the overall UX vision throughout the digital ecosystem. I play a critical role in driving the overall UX practice, help to develop team capabilities, and work as an evangelist and thought leader.

05. Production

In this phase, I create high-fidelity (hi-fi) design mockups and polish any animations that will be in the product. I create the digital assets that are required and validate design solutions (for both stakeholders and end-users) through user testing. I also in this phase collaborite with developers to guide the overall vision of the product through it's completion.

These steps may sound like a waterfall design process; however, I maintain communication and collaboration throughout. I try to constantly challenge assumptions and never ignore insights gained throughout the entire design process - even if that means drastically altering the product at any given point in the design workflow.
